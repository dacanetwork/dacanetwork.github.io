Background

Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (“DACA”) is the result of an executive order signed by President Obama. DACA granted approximately 700,000 undocumented immigrants, between the ages of 15-30, a Social Security Number, and an Employment Authorization Document; the latter of which must be renewed every two or three years. It also came as a relief to many of its recipients, as it gave them a chance to come out of the shadows and more vocally and closely integrate into society. Additionally, many DACA recipients were already well-educated, but simply lacked the work authorization to put their knowledge into action.

Many expect the new President to repeal DACA. The threat grows more real by the day, as the news reports the appointment of several anti-immigrant cabinet members. Those who benefit from DACA are productive and well-qualified members of American society, who contribute to the communities with which they have come to identify.
